About Me

Educator. Researcher. Builder.

I am a David Ross Fellow and PhD candidate in Curriculum & Instruction (Curriculum Studies) at Purdue University, where my research sits at the intersection of artificial intelligence and teacher education. I investigate how AI-driven tools and literacy can be integrated into teacher preparation programs to enhance both teaching efficacy and student learning outcomes.

Beyond the academy, I founded Brace-Up The Young — an education non-profit that has impacted over 7,000 students across five Nigerian states through programs in STEAM education, girls' schooling, and civic technology. I believe deeply that education is the most powerful lever for social transformation.

"My goal is to prepare learners not just for academic success, but to make meaningful contributions to society and create positive social impact."

My work has taken me from rural classrooms in Ogun State to research conferences at Purdue, from Morocco's Mohammed VI Polytechnic to Oxford's Saïd Business School. I bring the same conviction everywhere: every learner has unique strengths waiting to be unlocked.
